Statistics

What is most detrimental to a dancer's life?
In the survey made to several professionals of classical dance, as well as to dancers in their last grades of study, it was obtained that the greatest complication within this art is usually injuries and surgeries, almost doubling the percentage of answers in comparison with the rest of the options.

What are the most common injuries?
Injuries within ballet tend to be as varied, from the feet to the neck, due to the fact that it is one of the most physically demanding sports. However, injuries along the entire foot, calf and knees are more common.

What injuries have the professionals suffered?
As we can see in the graph obtained, professionals tend to suffer mostly from muscle overload, instep sprain, and Achilles tendon. The most common injuries are usually located in the lower leg, covering the entire area of the foot and calf.

Ways of preventing injuries
Classical dance professionals mostly agree that the best ways to prevent future injuries are conditioning exercises and the correct application of technique, which boils down to full physical awareness and self-care performance.

Causes of injury
In addition to the previous survey question, the main cause of injury is the incorrect application of technique, which makes sense, considering that its counterpart was selected as the best way to prevent injury.

Technology Utility

Opinions on technology applied in ballet
Most of the classical professionals, consider of great importance and usefulness the technology to learn new exercises or choreography, to keep the rhythm of the classes during the pandemic, and to be able to see the different classical works that are presented in theaters throughout the world. Judging by the opinions gathered, there is no product similar to ours on the market so far that focuses on the constant monitoring of movements and routines of the dancer, in order to prevent future injury.

What makes an application practical and user-friendly?

Technology areas in ballet
